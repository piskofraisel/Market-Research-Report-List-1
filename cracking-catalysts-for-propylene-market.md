<p><p>Cracking catalysts are essential for the production of propylene, a key building block in petrochemicals. In steam cracking, hydrocarbons are thermally decomposed at high temperatures, producing ethylene and propylene. Fluid catalytic cracking (FCC) utilizes catalysts to enhance the conversion of heavier feedstocks into lighter olefins, including propylene. Both processes are critical for meeting the growing demand for propylene in various applications, such as plastics and chemicals, by optimizing yield and efficiency in refining operations.</p></p>
